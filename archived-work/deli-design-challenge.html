This is the result of a design challenge from sharpen.design!













My prompt was to redesign a deli label, paying extra attention to design thinking. I approached this challenge by thinking of frustrations a customer may have ordering at a deli. My local deli has a decentralized system where they take orders arbitrarily, and then completed sandwiches are often mistakenly taken from the person who ordered it. Creating this system solves a lot of those issues.

Above I have shown my process and solution to this problem; the customer takes an empty slip and uses a hole puncher to select their ingredients. They rip off the number tag and then come back later to exchange the tag for the sandwich - which also eliminates the need to call for the customer’s name or an unusually long and specific order.
